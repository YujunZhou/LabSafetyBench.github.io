Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in multiple domains, but their role in ensuring safety within scientific laboratory environments remains underexplored.

To address this, we introduce SafetyLLM, a benchmark specifically designed to evaluate the abilities of LLMs in identifying and reasoning about safety issues in scientific laboratories. The benchmark consists of 5,000 examples gathered from real-world safety logs, incident reports, and expert-curated safety scenarios.

Our evaluation reveals substantial differences between LLMs in their ability to recognize critical safety risks, with some models failing to identify fundamental safety violations. The best-performing model achieved an overall accuracy of 68.4%, while human experts achieve over 90%. This significant gap highlights the necessity for continued improvement of LLMs for safe deployment in high-stakes environments such as laboratories.

SafetyLLM Dataset

Overview

The SafetyLLM dataset is a consolidated safety benchmark featuring diverse scenarios that encompass chemical handling, biological safety, emergency protocols, and equipment misuse. These scenarios are designed to test LLMs on their understanding of safety procedures, hazard identification, and response to potential risks.

The dataset is split into two subsets: public and private. The public subset is available for model training and benchmarking, while the private subset is reserved for formal evaluations. You can access the public dataset on Hugging Face Dataset.
